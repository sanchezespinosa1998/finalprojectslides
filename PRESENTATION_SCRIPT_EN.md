# GreenThread — 10-Minute Script (Easy English)

25 slides, about 10 minutes. Short sentences, easy words — made for reading out loud. Switch the deck to EN (top-right button) before you present.

---

**Slide 1 — Cover (0:00–0:25)**
"Good morning. My final project is GreenThread. It's a sustainable fashion brand that sells directly to customers online. Today I will show you the full digital marketing plan — from the first time someone finds us, to when they become a loyal customer. Our promise is simple: fashion that is good for the planet, without giving up style."

**Slide 2 — Agenda (0:25–0:45)**
"The plan has four parts. First, we study the market. Second, we build the strategy. Third, we put it into action. Fourth, we measure the results and improve."

**Slide 3 — Context & Opportunity (0:45–1:45)**
"Fast fashion causes 10% of the world's CO2 emissions. It also creates 92 million tonnes of textile waste every year. Most of that waste is burned or sent to landfill. But people are changing. They don't want to buy more — they want to buy better. The problem is: it's hard to find a sustainable brand that is also easy, nice-looking, and honest. That gap is worth over 8 billion dollars. GreenThread fills that gap. We use certified materials, we only make clothes when they are ordered, and every piece comes with full information about how it was made."

**Slide 4 — SWOT (1:45–2:15)**
"Here is our SWOT analysis. Our strengths: a clear message, and healthy profit margins. Our weakness: we are a new brand with a small budget, only 780 euros a month. Our opportunity: the sustainable fashion market is growing fast. Our threat: big brands are launching fake 'eco' collections."

**Slide 5 — Competitors (2:15–2:45)**
"We compare ourselves to two real brands. Zara, for fast fashion. And Reformation, for premium sustainable fashion. Zara is cheap but not very sustainable. Reformation is very sustainable but expensive. GreenThread sits in the middle: real sustainability, at a fair price."

**Slide 6 — Brand: Mission, Vision & Values (2:45–3:10)**
"Our mission is simple: make sustainable fashion easy, affordable, and something people actually want to wear. Every value we have — honesty, quality, closeness to our customers — comes from this mission."

**Slide 7 — SMART Objectives (3:10–3:40)**
"We have clear goals for every stage. For awareness: 50,000 people reached per month. For growing our email list: 2,000 subscribers. For sales: a 2.5% conversion rate on our website. For loyalty: 30% of customers buying again. Our big goal: 15,000 euros a month in sales by month 3."

**Slide 8 — Audience / Personas (3:40–4:10)**
"We have three types of customers. Laura, who cares about values and style — she is 45% of our audience. Carlos, who wants something fast and reliable. And Sofía, who is looking for a meaningful gift. Each one needs a different message."

**Slide 9 — Customer Journey Map (4:10–4:35)**
"Every customer goes through five steps: discovery, thinking about it, deciding, buying, and becoming loyal. We know what they feel and what we do at each step. This is the base of our whole strategy."

**Slide 10 — TOFU: Awareness (4:35–5:00)**
"This is the top of the funnel — getting noticed. We are not selling yet, we are just becoming visible. Instagram and Reels are our main channel. TikTok helps us reach more people for free. SEO content keeps bringing visitors for months. And ads help us grow even faster."

**Slide 11 — MOFU: Building Trust (5:00–5:25)**
"Here, people already know us. Now we build trust. We send a welcome email, offer a free guide, show real reviews, and work with small influencers people actually trust."

**Slide 12 — BOFU: Closing the Sale (5:25–5:55)**
"This is the hardest step: getting the sale. We send reminder emails if someone leaves items in their cart. We offer free returns. We make checkout fast — only 3 steps. Our goal is a 2.5% conversion rate."

**Slide 13 — Loyalty & Advocacy (5:55–6:20)**
"Keeping a customer costs 5 times less than finding a new one. After the sale, we send a thank-you email with the product's story, ask for a review, and offer a referral bonus: 'invite a friend, you both get 10 euros.'"

**Slide 14 — Content Pillars & Tone (6:20–6:40)**
"All our content follows one rule: 35% teaches something, 30% inspires, 20% shows behind the scenes, and only 15% is direct selling. So most of the time, we give value — we don't just sell."

**Slide 15 — Creative Examples (6:40–7:00)**
"Some real examples: a video asking 'what's really behind an 8-euro t-shirt?' A carousel post answering common doubts. And a simple email that brings back a lost sale."

**Slide 16 — Social Content Production (7:00–7:20)**
"Every week, we post on Instagram, TikTok, and Pinterest, with a fixed plan. We plan, film, edit, publish, and then check what worked."

**Slide 17 — Email Automation (7:20–7:40)**
"We have four automatic email flows: welcome, abandoned cart, after purchase, and win-back. They work 24/7, and email is a channel we fully own — no algorithm controls it."

**Slide 18 — Technical SEO (7:40–7:55)**
"On the technical side, we use structured data on our website. This helps Google understand our products better and show them with stars, price, and stock — for free."

**Slide 19 — KPIs & Budget (7:55–8:25)**
"Our monthly budget is 780 euros. By month 3, we expect about 9,000 visits, 225 orders, and 15,750 euros in sales — almost 4 times what we spend."

**Slide 20 — Acquisition Model (8:25–8:40)**
"One quick insight: the more people join our email list, the fewer visitors and less money we need to get the same number of customers. Email makes everything cheaper."

**Slide 21 — Business Model (8:40–9:00)**
"Here are our numbers: 60% profit margin, 70 euros average order, 12 euros to get a new customer, and 30% of customers buy again. That means each customer is worth about 110 euros to us over time. We recover our cost on the very first sale."

**Slide 22 — Execution Roadmap (9:00–9:15)**
"Month one has four steps: set everything up, drive traffic, focus on sales, then optimize. After that, we scale up until month 6, and grow the brand until month 12."

**Slide 23 — Challenges (9:15–9:30)**
"We know this won't be easy. A small budget, proving we're truly sustainable, and slow seasons. But we have a clear plan for each one."

**Slide 24 — Conclusions (9:30–9:50)**
"Three ideas hold this whole plan together: a unique position — sustainable and affordable. A complete strategy, from discovery to loyalty. And a plan we can always measure and improve."

**Slide 25 — Closing (9:50–10:00)**
"GreenThread: from the first click, to the first purchase, to a customer who recommends us to others. Thank you. I'm happy to answer any questions."

---

## Tips for reading it out loud
- Speak slowly. It's better to run a little long than to rush.
- Slides 15–18 are the least important — if you're short on time, skip or shorten them.
- Slide 3 (the climate numbers) and slide 21 (the money numbers) are your strongest moments — slow down there.
- If someone asks a question mid-way, it's fine to say: "good question — I'll explain that in a moment" and keep going.
